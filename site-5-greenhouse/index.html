// why grow instead of install

Installed software waits. Grown systems keep working.

Most "AI adoption" is a chatbot somebody has to remember to prompt — a plastic plant. A grown system has roots in your actual workflows and keeps photosynthesising whatever the day brings. That's the difference between a tool and a capability.

👁

Every inbound email read and triaged — critical, action, info, noise — every thirty minutes. Platform changes, competitor moves and research tracked without anyone asking.

↳ running on our own business since day one

↳ 95% data reduction, zero manual effort

LOGGED ✓

// how an engagement actually grows

Four seasons. Every engagement, in order.

We document client builds decision-by-decision — our "Building in Public" case study logged 14 formal decisions as they happened. The seasons map to the same gates every engagement passes through:

SEASON 1 · SOW — the audit

Find the one patch worth planting

One conversation about where your business actually is — tools, processes, costs, gaps. No greenfield fantasy; we work with your soil as we find it. If AI can't earn its keep, we say so and stop here. Cheapest "no" you'll ever buy.

SEASON 2 · GROW — the build

Prove it in your climate

A focused system against that one problem, on your data, on your infrastructure where it's sensitive. Designed so the first engagement costs less than the value it delivers — a rule, not a slogan.

SEASON 3 · PRUNE — the review

Let Kieran at it with the secateurs

Every build gets adversarial review — overclaims cut, failures documented. What survives pruning is what goes in the case study, blight and all.

SEASON 4 · HARVEST — the transfer

You keep everything that grew

Code, configuration, documentation, training — all of it transfers. The capability stays rooted in your business when we leave. You renew because it works, never because leaving hurts. No landlord.
